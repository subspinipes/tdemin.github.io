Releasing the Secure Messaging Scorecard

July 28, 2017

Timur Demin

Finding a good application to use for secure messaging nowadays may be a hard thing to do, because lots of them say they are secure and don’t snoop on you. The Electronic Frontier Foundation realised that and tried to make up a scorecard that would compare messengers and show which are good for security and which aren’t. This scorecard was a basic comparation that didn’t include lots of things necessary to decide on the actual security of an app.

This scorecard aims to include those details. Those are:

  • the open code. You should be able to investigate into anything you use for security;
  • the limitations of end-to-end encryption. Some apps have limited functionality when being used for end-to-end encryption on messages;
  • the user interface design. An ugly app won’t attract people to use it;
  • the architecture. Most of the solutions presented are centralized, but centralization sometimes isn’t good;
  • …and others.

So, this is it, the Secure Messaging Scorecard, enhanced by me.

Comments are welcome, please email me if you have any. ;)
