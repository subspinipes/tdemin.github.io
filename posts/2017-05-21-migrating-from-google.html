Migrating from Google, or The Hard Way Out

May 21, 2017

Timur Demin

I’ve never thought it would be really hard to stop using Google services until today.

I now have to somehow replace:

  • my calendar app. Google Calendar is a simple-to-use app, and its Goals is a real killer feature, as the schedule somehow adapts to you. The way reminders and stuff created via Google App integrate into your calendar is also a charm. I have no idea how do I replace it all, but using GNOME Calendar seems to be… sustainable?
  • task list app. Taskwarrior is fine, right, but, well, does it really integrate with GNOME desktop? Using it on Android is also questionable, as I want something simplier to use.
  • notes app. Do you know any free software which allows you to synchronize notes and reminders while integrating in your calendar and reminders? I don’t, so I have to use Google Keep, and I doubt I could replace it.
  • email. Well, that’s the least problem of all, however, I’ll still have to tune my new mailbox to be easy-to-use and filter stuff well, just like Gmail does.
  • printing system. I occasionally use Cloud Print, and I still don’t know a way to use CUPS on my phone.
  • my web browser. If I stop using Google services, I don’t have to use Chrome/Chromium either, right? Firefox is alright, I presume? Didn’t use it for years.
  • sync. I’m speaking primarily of Google Photos, which gives you unlimited storage for your pics. If I want to abandon Google, I would search for a decentralized analogue, not MEGA or something promising you to keep your files private. Well… perhaps I’ll find a good alternative some day.
  • Play Services. Anyone knows how do I receive WhatsApp notifications while using Doze and not using Play Services?

You know, migrating from proprietary services and software is hard, and that’s the reason you shouldn’t use them anyway. A proprietary ecosystem will take your stuff and won’t let you go away easily.
