Updating the Secure Messaging Scorecard—Features Are a Must

August 29, 2017

Timur Demin

The previous version of the Secure Messaging Scorecard did a good job on comparing the messengers on their ability on securely messaging people. So far this was the most important feature to compare the messengers on.

While secure messaging is a great point, I felt that the scorecard missed the point on the featurefulness of the messengers compared. Moreover, XMPP was highly praised and received a very good score, while its modern clients mostly miss convenient things like phone calls and voice messaging, the things popular messengers already have.

A full feature comparison would go away from secure messaging, so I decided to pick the most needful ones: sending files, audio and video calling.

This quickly changed things. XMPP, with having a few standards on sending files whilst having no mobile clients able to call, withdrew its position and fell back, allowing more usable messengers to get a better score and a better position.

So far so good, now it’s a much more honest comparison—it respects the features you want from an instant messaging client while retaining the main point it was created for, the secure messaging.

The update also includes a few score fixes. Google Allo finally received a desktop client (BTW it only works with Chrome), ChatSecure is known to have no support for encrypted group chats, etc.

As always, you’re welcome to email me, if you have any comments.
